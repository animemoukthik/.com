30. Mob Psycho 100

Mob Psycho 100 is a compelling anime series adapted from ONE's manga of the same name, known for its unique blend of supernatural action, humor, and character-driven storytelling. The plot revolves around Shigeo "Mob" Kageyama, a seemingly ordinary middle school boy with extraordinary psychic powers. Despite his immense abilities, Mob suppresses his emotions to maintain control, striving for a normal life and avoiding harm to others. He is guided by Reigen Arataka, a charismatic but fraudulent psychic mentor who helps Mob navigate the complexities of his powers while exploiting them for profit. Alongside them is Dimple, a mischievous spirit who evolves from antagonist to ally, adding depth to Mob's journey.

29. Sword Art Online

Sword Art Online follows the story of Kazuto "Kirito" Kirigaya, who, along with thousands of players, becomes trapped in a virtual reality MMORPG (Massively Multiplayer Online Role-Playing Game) called Sword Art Online. The players must clear the game's dungeons to escape, but dying in the game means dying in real life. Kirito, a skilled player, navigates through various challenges, forms alliances, and confronts adversaries as he strives to free himself and the other players from the deadly game. Sword Art Online is known for its blend of virtual reality,action-packed battles, and exploration of relationships in a digital world, making it a popular choice among anime enthusiasts.

28.Haikyuu!!

Haikyuu!! follows the journey of Shoyo Hinata, a determined and energetic high school student who dreams of becoming a volleyball champion despite his short stature. Inspired by a legendary player known as the "Little Giant," Hinata joins his school's volleyball team and forms a passionate rivalry with Tobio Kageyama, a talented but aloof setter. Together, they lead their team through intense matches and fierce competitions, learning valuable lessons about teamwork, perseverance, and sportsmanship along the way. Haikyuu!! is celebrated for its dynamic animation, engaging characters, and thrilling sports action, capturing the spirit of camaraderie and determination in competitive sports.

24. Gintama

In the early 17th century, Japan adopted strict isolation policies and severed almost all ties with foreign countries. In 1853, the US fleet commanded by Admiral Perry forced the doors open, leading to a modernization of the nation's sociopolitical systems and the eventual fall of the Shogunate. The samurai regime ended its life with the imperial restoration in 1868. That's what history tells, but in Gintama, that's not what happens. Instead of the US fleet, the country is stormed by spaceships coming from four corners of the universe and soon we see it occupied by extraterrestrials. The Shogunate has become a puppet of alien occupation armies. All samurais have been disarmed and reduced to temp workers with no skills who just try to make ends meet.

19.March Comes in Like a Lion

Having reached professional status in middle school, Rei Kiriyama is one of the few elite in the world of shogi. Due to this, he faces an enormous amount of pressure, both from the shogi community and his adoptive family. Seeking independence from his tense home life, he moves into an apartment in Tokyo. As a 17-year-old living on his own, Rei tends to take poor care of himself, and his reclusive personality ostracizes him from his peers in school and at the shogi hall. However, not long after his arrival in Tokyo, Rei meets Akari, Hinata, and Momo Kawamoto, a trio of sisters living with their grandfather who owns a traditional wagashi shop. Akari, the oldest of the three girls, is determined to combat Rei's loneliness and poorly sustained lifestyle with motherly hospitality. The Kawamoto sisters, coping with past tragedies, also share with Rei a unique familial bond that he has lacked for most of his life. As he struggles to maintain himself physically and mentally through his shogi career, Rei must learn how to interact with others and understand his own complex emotions.

18.Kotaro Lives Alone

In Karino's eyes, Kotarou is an odd kid: he speaks in an extremely formal manner, and he lives alone in his apartment-no parents or relatives in sight. But Kotarou neither seems to mind, nor wants to rely on people. Feeling sympathetic toward Kotarou's circumstances, Karino decides to follow Kotarou to a bathhouse in case something might happen to him and there he comes to understand the little boy is not so different from him. Little by little, Karino-along with the other residents of the apartment complex-grows fond of Kotarou and his antics. At the same time, Kotarou himself might have found something akin to a family in his unique neighbors.
